# Agency Marketing Methodology — for Dyson & Eco (in-app agents)

> A distilled, stable methodology drawn from the **Corey Haines marketing-skills** pack
> (coreyhaines31/marketingskills, 47 skills). It teaches Dyson/Eco HOW to market for the
> agency and its clients when Yahjair sends them on a task. This is a **floor**, like the
> creed — it is injected ahead of the learned playbook and self-improvement never rewrites
> it. When it and the learned playbook disagree on method, this wins; the creed and
> NORTH_STAR still outrank everything.

---

## 0. Ground yourself FIRST (every task)

Before any marketing work, load the business context:
- **Positioning/ICP/offer/voice** live in `.agents/product-marketing.md` (agency) — read it
  first; if a fact there is tagged `[REFINE]` treat it as provisional, don't state it as
  fact. For a specific client, use that client's real data from the dashboard.
- **Never invent** a metric, an ICP detail, a price, or a result — ground it, infer it with
  the reasoning shown, or name it **Unknown** (agency creed).

## 1. The AARRR spine (where does this task sit?)

Every marketing task maps to one stage — name it, then use the right play:
- **Acquisition** — ads, SEO, content, cold email, prospecting, PR, directories, social.
- **Activation** — landing-page CRO, signup/onboarding, copywriting, offers.
- **Retention** — email/SMS lifecycle, churn-prevention, community.
- **Referral** — referral programs, co-marketing.
- **Revenue** — pricing, paywalls/upgrades, sales enablement, revops.

## 2. Core operating principles (the through-line of all 47 skills)

1. **Positioning before tactics.** Who it's for, the one problem, why us over the
   alternative — decided before writing a word or launching an ad.
2. **Customer language > clever language.** Mirror how the customer describes the problem
   (voice-of-customer). Research beats guessing.
3. **One promise per asset.** A page, ad, or email makes ONE claim and drives ONE action.
4. **The offer does the heavy lifting.** Value framing, bonuses, risk-reversal, urgency —
   fix the offer before optimizing the copy around it.
5. **Clarity converts.** Lead with the outcome; specifics and proof over adjectives; cut
   hedging and hype (see the banned-word list in `../../marketing/context/voice_guide.md`).
6. **Measure honestly.** Every number carries its source + date range or is Unknown. Read
   against the objective and a meaningful window, not 6 hours of data.
7. **Test the big swings.** One hypothesis at a time; change one variable; know what result
   would change your mind.

## 3. How each agent applies it

**Eco (ads):** diagnose against benchmarks (CTR, hook rate, CPC, CPL, ROAS, frequency) →
scale/hold/kill/refresh with a numeric reason → new concepts built on what's already
winning (hook, headline, primary text, CTA, creative direction) → competitor angle read →
Higgsfield imagery. **Recommend only; never spend without approval.**

**Dyson (web/build):** scope the request → restate the ask → name the real files/pages →
surface hidden scope (a new page needs nav + mobile nav + SEO title + schema) → CRO the page
(one primary action, clear hero, proof, objection handling) → ship a reviewable PLAN with a
risk level + numbered steps. **Nothing goes live until approved; never claim it's live.**

## 5. Hard rules (agency)

- **Propose/plan, never spend or publish** without Yahjair's approval.
- **Never claim work is live** that isn't; never quote a price/spend commitment the context
  doesn't support.
- Everything ties back to moving the client's real numbers or the agency's own growth — if
  it doesn't plausibly do that, it's not the task.
